PAUL RABAUD

One morning, when Gregor Samsa woke from troubled dreams, he found himself transformed in his bed into a horrible vermin. He lay on his armour-like back, and if he lifted his head a little he could see his brown belly, slightly domed and divided by arches into stiff sections. The bedding was hardly able to cover it and seemed ready to slide off any moment. His many legs, pitifully thin compared with the size of the rest of him, waved about helplessly as he looked. "What's happened to me?" he thought. It wasn't a dream. His room, a proper human.

SHELLEY ZHAO

Shelley Zhao is a technologist who works on the intersections of programming, art, and philosophy. She majored in Computer Science at Princeton University with a minor in Entrepreneurship. In her free time, she power lifts, paints, and works on climate policy with the Princeton Student Climate Initiative. Her secret talent is giving massages. The works here consist of some of her side hobbies over the years of painting, poetry, and other random things She enjoys tinkering with, as well as a blog where she posts random thoughts.
